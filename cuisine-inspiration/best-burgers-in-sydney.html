 5 of the best burgers in Sydney

  • 3 July 2017
  • Editor
  • Read time: 3 min
sydney burgers american
Share this article

Watching the game with your pals? Need something hearty after a long day? Sometimes, you just want a burger. And if you're having one of those days when only an epic burger will do, look no further – we've rounded up some of the best burger joints in Sydney.

Any of these beauties will wow you with their authentic ingredients, original combinations and generous fillings.

1. Cheese, Burger Project

A post shared by BURGER PROJECT (@burgerproject) on Mar 29, 2017 at 2:24pm PDT

Burger Project swept up 2015's 'best Australian burger café', so you know the eats here are going to be awesome. They take their burgers seriously, with 100% of their beef coming from Cape Grim, Tasmania. – the best recipe for quality burgers. Their classic cheeseburger is where it's at, though – just a juicy beef patty, cheese, onions, pickles, tomato, fresh lettuce and plenty of secret sauce.

Where: Burger Project, various locations

2. Katsu Chicken Burger, Thirsty Bird

A post shared by Anna & Phillip (@snap.eat.share) on Apr 22, 2016 at 5:05am PDT

At Thirsty Bird, traditional Buffalo-style crispy battered chicken is at the core of every item on the menu. Slow cooking is the name of the game here – the meat is first seasoned in salt, garlic and herbs, then air dried for 24 hours for extra crispness. Lastly, they coat the chicken with their house mix of flour and spices, dip it twice in a buttermilk bath and deep fry it till it's golden and crunchy. Our favourite is their Japanese-inspired Katsu Chicken Burger, breaded with panko and slathered with kewpie mayonnaise and fruity tonkatsu sauce.

Where: Thirsty Bird, Potts Point

3. Lamb Smash, Smashed Burger

A post shared by Smashed Burger (@smashedburgerau) on Jun 7, 2016 at 6:10pm PDT

At Smashed Burger you will find the tenderest meat ever. What's their secret? It's in the name with this one – they smash it. First they go to town with a tenderizer, slow roast it for half a day, and then give it a final smash on the griddle as it's grilling.  But it's not just about your beef and chicken regulars here. You'll find pork and lamb, vegetarian-friendly-falafel and even a bit of salmon. We love the Lamb Smash, where rich lamb is topped with red onion, rocket, tomato, bacon and a creamy yoghurt sauce.

Where: Smashed Burger, Woolloomooloo

4. Hamburgesa, Barrio Cellar

A post shared by Barrio Cellar (@barriocellar) on Feb 1, 2017 at 3:58pm PST

 Chill out and enjoy Barrio Cellar's burgers. This late night tequileria and eatery serves Mexican and Mezcal street food, including burgers, botanas (snacks) and quesadillas. Try their Hamburgesa, a cheeseburger with red onions, pickled jalapeno, chimichurri mayo and chipotle chilli mustard. Or kick things up a notch with their innovative Sweet Bacon Blue Burger, with a premium pasture-fed beef patty, bacon, dulce de leche and blue cheese mayo.

Where: Barrio Cellar, Central

5. Chee-Ze, Ze Pickle

A post shared by Ze Pickle (@zepickle) on Sep 17, 2016 at 6:21pm PDT

These burgers have been voted 'the best burgers on the Gold Coast' for two years in a row, so you know it's the good stuff here if the surfers love it. Ze Pickle's Sydney outpost features top-quality burgers made with Wagyu beef. This exclusive Japanese breed has higher level of fat marbling than any other cattle, making the meat extra rich and juicy. The most popular choice here is the Chee-Ze – a bun filled with a hand-pressed Wagyu beef patty, maple-smoked bacon, fried onion straws, cheese, salad leaves and tomato, all covered with the home sauce.

Where: Ze Pickle, Surry Hills
